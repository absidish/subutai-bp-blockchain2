Subutai: Great Internet Mersenne Prime Search

Subutai helping science!

The Great Internet Mersenne Prime Search (or: GIMPS) is a collaborative project of volunteers who are dedicating their idle computing resources to Search for mersenne prime numbers.

The GIMPS project is interesting because it was the first such collaborative effort on the Internet, founded in 1997. Since then, GIMPS has found a total of 16 Mersenne primes. This may not sound as much, but considering only 50 has ever been found and that 14 of those 16 at the time they were found were the largest known prime numbers.

GIMPS achieve this collaborative efford by providing a piece of software known as Prime95. While there is nothing complicated about installing Prime95, Subutai is all about sharing otherwise idle resources, and thus makes it the perfect platform on which to deploy Prime95.

This page describes in details how to deploy Prime95 on the Subutai platform.

Sign up

Head to Get Started/Create Account on mersenne.org and register a new account. Remember your Login as you will need this later. Once logged in, your name will be displayed in a green box in the upper right hand corner.

Deploy GIMPS Blueprint

First sign in to the Subutai Bazaar. If this is your first time using Subutai, follow the steps here to sign up and add necessary peers. For this blueprint, no domain is necessary.

Now select Products in the left hand side menu and select the GIMPS Blueprint.

Click the blue Build button.

Fill in the form with the appropriate information. Most important is that the primeUser variable must match the login name that you were choosing when your registered on mersenne.org.

Since this blueprint does not require any incoming port mappings the next page is empty. Simply click the Next button and you will be taken to the peer selection form.

Select a peer with idle resources - preferably of course one of your own. Click "Next" and finally on the last page of the wizard press the Build button.

After a few minutes your new Subutai Environment should be ready and running and you are now contributing to the search for ever larger Prime numbers. Depending on which hostname you chose, after a few minutes you should see your new CPU show up in the statistics on mersenne.org:
